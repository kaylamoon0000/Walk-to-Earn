Proposal

AI Walk-to-Earn Harlem is a public-space activation platform designed to increase pedestrian movement, support small businesses, and improve the experience of Harlem Plaza. The project combines route personalization, walking verification, landmark checkpoints, and reward systems in one user-friendly experience.

Project Overview

The platform rewards users for walking along recommended plaza routes, reaching checkpoints near local businesses, and verifying those visits through QR-style interactions. Its goal is to transform the plaza from a pass-through space into an active destination where movement, public health, and local economic activity reinforce one another.

Problem Definition

Many plazas feel underused because they do not provide strong reasons to stay, explore, or interact with nearby businesses. Small businesses may sit next to foot traffic without truly benefiting from it. This platform addresses that gap by making walking visible, measurable, and rewarding.

Site / Context

The design responds to movement patterns, comfort zones, safety, accessibility, use patterns, and business activity. Direct paths become guided loops, underused corners become checkpoint destinations, and nearby storefronts become active parts of the pedestrian experience.

Final Redesign Proposal

The selected direction combines route guidance, checkpoint verification, and reward personalization. Users see a clear path, businesses get measurable participation data, and the plaza gains a stronger public identity through visible activity and destination-based walking.

Human Reflection on AI Use

AI helps generate route options, reward logic, visualizations, and interaction models. Human judgment is still needed to decide where checkpoints belong, how safe and inclusive routes should be, and how to balance public-space goals with business needs.

Why this version matters

This platform version moves beyond a static proposal website. It demonstrates how the concept would behave as a functional system with maps, checkpoints, tracking, leaderboards, business integration, and a user-friendly interface that makes the idea feel buildable.
