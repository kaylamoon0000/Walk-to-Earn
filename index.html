Proposal

AI Walk-to-Earn Harlem is a public-space activation platform designed to increase pedestrian movement, support small businesses, and improve the experience around Montefiore Square. It combines route personalization, account-based progress, walking verification, checkpoints, and reward systems in one unified flow.

Project Overview

The platform rewards users for walking along recommended routes, reaching checkpoints near businesses, and verifying those visits through QR-style interactions. The goal is to transform the space from a pass-through corridor into an active public destination with measurable participation.

Problem Definition

Many public spaces do not create strong reasons to stay, explore, or support nearby stores. This platform responds by making movement visible, measurable, and directly tied to local business participation and user account progress.

Site / Context

The design responds to movement patterns, accessibility, comfort, safety, and business activity. Direct paths become guided routes, underused segments become destinations, and nearby storefronts become active parts of the public-space experience.

Final Proposal

The chosen direction combines route guidance, real business checkpoints, account-based saved progress, reward personalization, and a trackable user journey. Businesses gain measurable participation data while users gain a richer urban walking experience.
